World 2: Rollout Strategy

Level 2.1: Chronological Targeting Plan

+

Primary Target: "Restaurant Fatima" (Food Shops)

Justification: The QR code menu feature provides immediate, standalone value for in-house dining. This breaks the "chicken-and-egg" problem by giving vendors a reason to adopt the platform *before* the consumer marketplace has users. It's our Trojan horse.

Level 2.2: Vendor Onboarding

+

Strategy: The Ground Game

  • Goal: Onboard the first 20 food shops in one dense, specific neighborhood.
  • Tactic 1: Founder-Led Sales. No one else has the passion. Founders make the first sales.
  • Tactic 2: The 3 PM Visit. Physically visit restaurants between lunch and dinner rush.
  • Tactic 3: The 5-Minute Demo. Don't talk, show. Have a tablet ready. Lead with the QR code menu demo.
  • Tactic 4: On-the-Spot Onboarding. Sign them up, help them upload 5 menu items. Leave them with a live system.

Level 2.3: Customer Acquisition

+

Strategy: Hyper-Local Activation

  • Goal: Acquire the first 100 active customers in the *same* neighborhood.
  • Condition: Begin only after 10+ vendors are live.
  • Tactic 1: In-Store Marketing. Provide all partners with branded table tents and stickers with a QR code to download the Hanuut app. This is our #1 channel.
  • Tactic 2: Geo-Fenced Social Ads. Run hyper-targeted (1-2km radius) ads featuring photos of the actual partner restaurants.
  • Tactic 3: Launch Offer. "Get 50% Off Your First Order" to drive initial trial.

Level 2.4: Founding Partner Program

+

The Offer for the First 10-20 Vendors:

  • Zero Commission: 0% commission on all marketplace orders for the first 6 months.
  • VIP Support: A private WhatsApp/Telegram group with the founders for instant support.
  • Feature Prioritization: Their feedback goes to the top of the development queue.
  • Co-Branded Marketing: We feature their story on our blog and social media.

World 4: Feedback & Iteration

Level 4.1: Feedback Mechanisms

+
  • 1. Vendor VIP Channel (Qualitative): Private WhatsApp/Telegram group for Founding Partners for raw, unfiltered feedback.
  • 2. In-App Customer Surveys (Quantitative): After a customer's 3rd order, trigger a simple Net Promoter Score (NPS) pop-up.
  • 3. Analytics Deep Dive (Behavioral): Weekly reviews of analytics dashboards to track user flows and drop-off points.
  • 4. Founder Conversations (Direct): Founders must personally call/visit every vendor monthly to ask "What do you love?" and "What do you hate?"

Level 4.2: Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

+

Top 5 KPIs for First 3 Months:

  1. Vendor Activation Rate: % of vendors who sign up AND get their first order within 7 days.
  2. Average Orders per Active Vendor (weekly): Is the platform generating real business for them?
  3. Customer Week 1 to Week 4 Retention: Do first-time customers come back? The ultimate test of PMF.
  4. Order Fulfillment Rate: % of submitted orders successfully completed. Checks operational health.
  5. Marketplace Liquidity: % of app visitors who successfully place an order. Measures supply/demand health.
