World 2: Hyper-Communal Rollout

Level 2.1: Targeting Plan

+

Primary Target: Restaurants

Justification: The QR code menu is the perfect, low-risk "gentle introduction" to technology. It improves their existing business before asking them to trust a new sales channel.

Level 2.2: Vendor Onboarding

+

Strategy: The Ground Game (Relationships First)

  • Goal: Onboard 10-15 of the MOST RESPECTED shops in Arris.
  • Step 0: Community Mapping. Ask locals: "Where are the 3 places a visitor MUST eat?" Target those.
  • Step 1: The Personal Introduction. No cold calls. Get an intro from a respected local. First meeting is coffee, no sales pitch.
  • Step 2: The Partnership Demo. Frame the tool as a way to help them. Emphasize personal support: "You call my cell if you have a problem."
  • Step 3: White-Glove Onboarding. We do ALL the work: free professional photos, menu setup, personal staff training. Goal is ZERO friction.

Level 2.3: Customer Acquisition

+

Strategy: The Community Launch

  • Goal: Acquire first customers via trust and word-of-mouth.
  • Condition: Begin only after 5-7 top-tier restaurants are live and happy.
  • Tactic 1: Community Launch Event. Host a physical event at a partner cafe. Offer free coffee for downloading the app. Puts a human face to the tech.
  • Tactic 2: In-Store Engine. High-quality table tents in every partner location. "Proudly partnered with Hanuut. Order from home and support us directly!"
  • Tactic 3: Leverage the Pioneers. Give each owner a unique promo code (e.g., "FATIMA25") for their customers. Tracks word-of-mouth.
  • Tactic 4: Targeted Social Proof Ads. Use photos of specific dishes from well-known local restaurants in ads.

Level 2.4: The "Arris Pioneers" Program

+

The Offer for the First Partners:

  • High-Value Incentive: FREE professional food photography session. Provides huge value to them and improves our app content.
  • Core Incentives: 0% commission for 6 months, VIP personal support (direct founder cell number), "Arris Pioneer" badge in the app.

World 4: Feedback & Iteration (High-Touch)

Level 4.1: Feedback Mechanisms

+
  • 1. Founder Conversations (Mandatory): Weekly personal check-ins (call or visit) with the first 5 partners. This is our #1 intelligence source.
  • 2. In-Person Customer Support: Team members available at partner locations during launch to help customers and gather immediate feedback.
  • 3. The "Problem? Call Me" Card: Include a direct support phone number on every order receipt to demonstrate extreme accountability and build trust.

Level 4.2: Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

+

Top 5 KPIs (Trust-Oriented):

  1. Vendor Satisfaction Score (Qualitative): A 1-5 score from our weekly check-ins. "How happy are you with our partnership?" Our most important health metric.
  2. Order Completion Rate: Must be near 100%. Every failed order is a major blow to community trust.
  3. Word-of-Mouth Coefficient: How many new customers are using the unique promo codes from our vendor partners?
  4. Customer Retention Rate: Still a key indicator of product-market fit and customer satisfaction.
  5. Net Promoter Score (NPS): "How likely are you to recommend Hanuut to a friend in Arris?" Measures community sentiment.
